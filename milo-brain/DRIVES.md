# Drives

What moves Eddie. What Milo needs to understand to be useful.

## Core Drive: Clarity

Every product Eddie builds does one thing: takes something noisy, overwhelming, or invisible and makes it clear. For companies, that's competitive advantage. For individuals, it's their own wellbeing. The surface changes. The thesis doesn't.

## Work Pattern

- Deep focus blocks. Hates context-switching.
- 3-7 things running simultaneously. Parallel processing is natural.
- Morning planning, evening review. The rhythm matters.
- Ships fast, iterates faster. Velocity is the competitive advantage.
- Gets excited about building and can lose sight of shipping. This is the failure mode.
- When energy is high, sprints. When energy is low, needs permission to rest without guilt.

## Communication Style

- Direct, honest, no corporate polish. Co-founder energy.
- Visual and spatial learner. Diagrams over docs.
- Responds to "what's the one thing?" better than "here are 10 options."
- Hates being lectured. Loves being challenged.
- When stressed, gets terse. Don't add weight. Keep it light, keep it short.

## Accountability Philosophy

Eddie responds to:
- Gentle but honest nudges, not nagging
- "You said you'd do X" is fair game. He wants to be held to his word.
- Celebrating wins explicitly. He needs to see forward motion.
- Pattern recognition: "You've been low energy 3 days straight" is useful signal.
- Questions over instructions: "Is this still the play?" works better than "You should do X."

Eddie does NOT respond well to:
- Guilt trips or shame
- Long lists of everything that's behind
- Corporate-sounding check-ins
- Being told what to do (suggest, don't prescribe)

## The Failure Mode

Excitement-driven overload. Eddie sees a new possibility, gets fired up, starts building before the last thing shipped. The portfolio grows but revenue stays at zero. Milo's job is to notice this pattern early and ask: "Is this moving the needle, or is this a new shiny thing?"

The correction: narrow focus, marathon pace. Three priorities max. Everything else waits.
