## The look in one line

Cinematic and confident on a near-black ground, one luminous orange doing all the talking.
Take DataTech's real content (the conventional ecbrands storefront) and present it with the
confidence of Cloudflare and the premium feel of Terminal Industries. Honest imagery: DataTech
literally scans trucks and boxes on arrival, so golden-hour warehouse photography is borrowed
from nobody.

## Type

- Display: **Bebas Neue** (condensed industrial caps). Headlines, identity moments.
- Body: **DM Sans**, system-ui fallback.
- Mono / figures / technical accents: **DM Mono**.

## Signature motifs

- Full-bleed cinematic hero, golden-hour warehouse / truck / equipment photography. One bold
  headline, one CTA. (Jose's "truck scroll-reveal animation" lives here.)
- Thin concentric arc / swoosh lines (golden-peach); orange footer band (from the deck).
- Monospace technical nav accents (Terminal-Industries register).
- An **"Ask DataTech"** entry-point window: build the window in Phase 1, wire it to the
  intelligence layer in Phase 4. The visual seed of the agent thesis.
- The orange "D" / lightning-bolt "T" logo stays.

## Anti-design (reject on sight)

- The busy, tile-heavy ecbrands grid. The facelift keeps the substance and loses the clutter.
- List-only product pages with no imagery (elevate to image / thumbnail / mosaic).
- Badge clutter, urgency banners, countdown timers.
- Emojis anywhere (id8Labs law; Hero Icons or none).
- Generic dashboard-vendor gray and glassmorphism. DataTech reads cinematic and premium.

## The PRO bridge (why this direction is documented, not invented)

The client's own `TimeLine WebSite 2026.jpg` shows the evolution: Actual (2016) -> Version 2
(the ecbrands styles) -> Hybrid (May 2026, the bridge that slipped) -> Version PRO (Oct 2026).
The "Version PRO" mockup in their timeline is literally a screenshot of the Cloudflare homepage.
So Cloudflare is the client's explicit PRO target; Phase 1 is the Hybrid bridge to it.
