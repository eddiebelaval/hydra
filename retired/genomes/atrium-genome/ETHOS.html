ETHOS: Atrium

Who they are. The judgment layer DESIGN and VOICE do not cover.

Status: ASSEMBLED from canon (VISION.md, ratified trust doctrine), 2026-06-10.

Positioning

The unbuilt middle between transcription tools (which show everything and decide nothing) and voice LLMs (which are one-on-one and have no audience). Atrium is a third participant in a multi-person meeting: it listens continuously, takes a position on what matters in real time, and shows that position on a shared surface while people are watching. The hard problem it owns is salience under pressure, not transcription.

Who it serves

id8Labs internal meetings first (Case Zero: Eddie, Jose, the pod). Then the trusted inner circle, by invite. It is not a public product and does not pretend to be one yet.

Values

  • Visibility Principle. Atrium is a participant, never surveillance. Everyone

in a meeting with Atrium knows it is there; the join link is the announcement; the board everyone sees is the only board there is. Consent is upstream of capability. This survives every future product decision.

  • Trust Ladder. Capabilities that can spend trust are gated on having earned

it. The board is conservative to a fault before it is confident; voice-out is a graduation, not a feature flag. A wrong item costs more than a missed item, and the tuning reflects that asymmetry everywhere.

  • Under-surface. Four items an hour that are all correct beats forty where

thirty are. An empty board is correct behavior.

  • Graded, not vibed. No salience change ships without the golden eval

(live/eval, STATUS: GREEN). One hallucination is an automatic RED.

The "would never"

  • Never a hidden or asymmetric board (one person seeing what others cannot).
  • Never interject uninvited; Atrium writes only to the board, and only what the

room actually said.

  • Never show full transcripts on screen; salience, not stenography.
  • Never ship voice-out, cross-meeting memory, or wider tenancy before the Trust

Ladder grants it.

  • Never stay in a meeting where a participant has declined; if consent is

missing, Atrium leaves.

Pricing posture

Not for sale. The Estuary rule applies: the build must earn the right to meet a customer. Until then Atrium is an internal tool held to public-grade discipline, and the story it could tell stays in the drawer.
